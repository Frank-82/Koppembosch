Prior to uptake in a feedlot, cattle are mostly kept on pastures of farmlands or on planted grain fields such as green wheatpasture. Once cattle reach the necessary absorbing mass, namely about 300 kg (650 pounds),they are transferred to a feedlot to follow a specialized diet of hay, maize, sorghum and various other cereals, by-products of food processing, such as sugar beet waste,molasses, soybean meal, animal bee products[3] or cotton seed flour, and minerals. In the U.S. northwest and Canada,barley, low-grade hardcover, chickpea, oats and on occasion potatoes are used as nutrition. Feedlot diets are usually very high in nutritional energy, to promote the build-up of fat, or meat marble (muscle fat). This fat may be sought after among certain consumers, as it promotes the 'juiciness' of the meat. The animal can put on 180 kg (400 pounds) during the 3â€“4 month stay in the feedlot.
